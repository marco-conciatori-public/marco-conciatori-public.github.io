What Mantis Can Do

Omnidirectional Movement

Mantis has four mecanum wheels, which grant it the ability to move forward, backward, sideways, and rotate on the spot. This omnidirectional movement allows it to navigate tight spaces and complex environments with ease. Mecanum wheels work best on surfaces that are not too smooth, nor too uneven.

For precise control, the speed can be adjusted in real time between 10% and 100% of the maximum velocity using the controller. This affects all form of movement, including autonomous navigation and arm speed.

Robotic Arm & Gripper

The 6-axis robotic arm provides a wide range of motion, mimicking the flexibility of a human arm. It can reach objects in various orientations and positions.

The arm is equipped with a parallel gripper that can handle objects of different shapes and sizes. The gripping force is adjustable to handle both delicate and robust items.

Computer Vision

Mantis uses a high-resolution camera and advanced computer vision algorithms to perceive its environment. This allows it to perform tasks like object recognition, tracking, and navigation.

  • Object Detection: Identifies and locates objects using a pre-trained YOLOv5 model.
  • AprilTag Following: Precisely follows visual markers for docking and guidance.
  • 3D Environment Mapping: Creates a 3D map of its surroundings using its depth camera.
